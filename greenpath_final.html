The Challenge

Nearby, but not always
reachable.

For many older adults in Melbourne, daily destinations are physically close — but walking there can feel uncertain, tiring, or even unsafe. These are the real barriers that stand between older adults and the independence they deserve.

☀️

Too Hot, Too Sunny

Summer heat makes walking difficult, and ordinary maps won't tell you which paths have shade.

🚶

Unsure About the Distance

Not knowing if there's somewhere to rest mid-walk stops many people from going out at all.

🏥

What If Something Goes Wrong?

Feeling unwell mid-walk without knowing where the nearest pharmacy is creates real anxiety.

🗺️

Ordinary Maps Fall Short

Google Maps shows the fastest route — not the most comfortable one for older adults.

Two Choices, Two Outcomes

Regular Walking vs. Staying Indoors

Two choices that lead to very different physical and mental outcomes.

🚶 Regular Walking
  • ✓ Healthier heart and lower blood pressure
  • ✓ Better mood, less anxiety and worry
  • ✓ Social connection and less loneliness
  • ✓ Deeper, more restful sleep
  • ✓ More flexible, joints stay mobile
🏠 Staying Sedentary
  • ✗ Increased risk of heart disease
  • ✗ Higher anxiety and low mood
  • ✗ Social isolation and loneliness
  • ✗ Poorer sleep quality
  • ✗ Reduced mobility and stiffness
"

What Research Tells Us

The Benefits of Walking, Backed by Data

❤️
19%
Reduction in Coronary Heart Disease Risk

Walking ~30 minutes a day, 5 days a week — based on a meta-analysis of nearly 300,000 participants. No gym, no equipment. Just walking.

Zheng et al., European Journal of Preventive Cardiology, 2009

👥
Walking Connects You to People

A wave to a neighbour, sitting in the park, a cup of tea at a café — these small moments are among the most effective ways to combat loneliness.

😌
Anxiety Symptoms Significantly Reduced

A meta-analysis of 770 older adult participants confirmed that regular physical activity significantly reduces anxiety and depression — with longer duration showing stronger effects.

BMC Sports Science, Medicine and Rehabilitation, 2024
